Work with Claim Payments

 

 

This area gives the user a quick review of previous payments. NetTracer provides the user with a summary of previous payments starting with the date the payment was made.

 

To add a Claim Payment to a Claim Resolution:

  1. Display the Claim Resolution screen for a Missing Articles report.
     

  2. Scroll down to the Payment Summary section and click to display the Add Payment section.
     

  3. Enter the any optional and required information in the appropriate fields.
     

  4. Click to add the Payment details to the Claim Resolution.

Important

 

Once a payment is added to a Claim Resolution, it can only be modified but not deleted.

 

 

To change or modify a Claim Payment on a Claim Resolution:

  1. Display the Claim Resolution screen for a Missing Articles report.
     

  2. Click the Modify link associated with the Payment Summary entry to display the Edit Payment section.
     

  3. Make the desired changes to the payment information.
     

  4. Click to accept the changes and close the Edit Payment section.
     

Claim Payment Fields

Note

 

An asterisk (*) indicates the field is a required field.

 

Tip

 

Text box fields can be edited by typing over the existing data with new data. Dropdown list fields can be changed by moving your cursor over the new option to highlight it and clicking to enter the new data in the field.

 

Field Name

Field Description

Date

The Date the payment section is completed is automatically entered by NetTracer.

Payment Location

Location is selected from the drop down box.  The options are Station, CBS (LZ) or Claim Office.

Payment Type

Payment Type is also selected from a drop down box.  Here the options are Damage, Delivery, Interim, Goodwill, Loss, Missing Articles, Unchecked Lost and Found or Other.

Comments

A Comments section is provided to record additional information regarding the claim settlement.

Draft Number

Draft Number is the area where the user will record the Draft or Check number.

Draft Request Date

Draft Request Date is provided for record keeping.  This is important when one office requests the payment and the draft/check is issued from another location.

Draft Issued Date

Draft Issued Date provides the user with an audit trail of when the draft or check was actually issued.

Draft Amount

Draft Amount will be the amount of payment being sent to the customer.

Currency

Currency gives the user the ability to record payment in various world currencies.

Voucher Amount

Voucher Amount entries are provided to record payments other than check or cash.

Mileage Amount

Mileage Amount entries are provided to record payments other than check or cash.

Code

The user may Code the payment from the drop down box using options of Advanced (Interim Charges), Delivery (Delivery Costs), Final (Final Settlement), Insurance (To record payments made by your insurance carrier or Risk Management office) and Other.
