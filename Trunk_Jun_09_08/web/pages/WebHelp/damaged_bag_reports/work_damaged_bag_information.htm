Work with Damaged Bag Information

 

 

Separate Damaged Bag Information is recorded for each bag that the passenger is reporting as damaged. Damaged Bag Information contains descriptive information about the extent of damage to each bag.

 

To add a damaged bag to a Damaged Bag report:

  1. Create a new Damaged Bag report or locate and view an existing one.
     

  2. Click the button to jump to the Damaged Bag Information section of the report.
     

  3. Click to add an additional bag to the report.
     

  4. Enter the optional and required information in the appropriate fields.
     

  5. Attach digital photos to the report, if available.
     

  6. Move on to the next section or scroll to the end of the report and click .

Note

 

There is no limit to the number of damaged bags that can be added to a Damaged Bag report.

 

 

To edit an existing damaged bag in a Damaged Bag report:

  1. Locate and view an existing Damaged Bag report.
     

  2. Click the button to jump to the Damaged Bag Information section of the report.
     

  3. Make the desired changes to the damaged bag's information fields.
     

  4. Scroll to the end of the report and click to accept the changes.
     

To delete a damaged bag from a Damaged Bag report:

  1. Locate and view an existing Damaged Bag report.
     

  2. Click the button to jump to the Damaged Bag Information section of the report.
     

  3. Click the button that corresponds with the bag that is to be deleted.
     

  4. Scroll to the end of the report and click to accept the changes.

Important

 

A minimum of one Damage Bag Information field is required to complete a report.

 

 

To upload a digital photo of a bag to a Damaged Bag report:

  1. Create a new Damaged Bag report or locate and view an existing one.
     

  2. Click the button to jump to the Damaged Bag Information section of the report.
     

  3. Click to upload a photo.
     

  4. Choose the file name of the photo you want to add and click .
     

  5. Click to upload the digital photo to the report.
     

  6. Move on to the next section or scroll to the end of the report and click to accept the changes.

Note

 

Repeat steps 3 through 5 to add additional photos.

 

Tip

 

Click on the thumbnail of the photo to enlarge the image.

 

 

To delete a digital photo from a Damaged Bag report:

  1. Locate and view an existing Damaged Bag report.
     

  2. Click the button to jump to the Damaged Bag Information section of the report.
     

  3. Click below the desired image to remove the photo from the report.
     

  4. Scroll to the end of the report and click to accept the changes.
     

Damaged Bag Information Fields

Note

 

An asterisk (*) indicates the field is a required field.

 

Tip

 

Text box fields can be edited by typing over the existing data with new data. Dropdown list fields can be changed by moving your cursor over the new option to highlight it and clicking to enter the new data in the field.

 

Field Name

Field Description

Claim Check Number

Enter the claim check number for each bag that has not been received by the passenger. If the claim check number is not known, enter the airline code of the carrier that originally checked the baggage.

Name on Bag

 

Enter the name or initials on the bag if it is different than the passenger’s name.

Color

 

Select the color of the bag by pointing to the color in the drop-down list and clicking on the selected color.  Alternatively, the color can be selected by placing the cursor in the field and entering the first letter of the desired color. For example, repeatedly entering the letter B will in order select Black, Blue, Beige and then Brown.

Type

Select the baggage type by pointing to the type in the drop-down list and clicking on the selected type. Alternatively, the type can be selected by placing the cursor in the field and entering the first digit of the desired bag type. For example, repeatedly entering the number 2 will in order select 20 through 29. For bag types 1-9 select 0 as the beginning number.

Descriptive Elements

Three (3) drop down list are provided for entering External Descriptive Elements. The selections in each box are identical; W-wheels, S-straps, P-pockets, H-handle, C-combination lock, B-single item in a box and X for no external descriptive elements. These may be entered by pointing to the descriptive element in the drop-down list and clicking on the selected element or by placing the cursor in the field and entering the first letter of the descriptive element; W, S, P, H, C, B or X.

Manufacturer

 

Select the name of the luggage manufacturer by pointing to the name in the drop-down list and clicking on the selected manufacturer.

Contents

 

Place your cursor in the contents field and enter a description of the item and the damage for any content items that incurred damage.

Level of Damage

 

Select the level of damage to the bag by pointing to Minor, Major or Complete in the drop-down list and clicking on the selected level of damage.

Damage Description

 

Enter a brief description of the damage to the bag such as which area or areas of the bag were damaged and what type of damage occurred.

Resolution

 

Enter information on the agreement you reached with the passenger to repair or replace the bag and what procedures need to be followed to resolve the damage baggage claim. If the damage claim was denied, enter the reason for the denial.

Cost

 

Enter the total dollar amount that the passenger is claiming for the original cost of the bag and the value of any items that might have been damaged inside the bag.

Currency

 

Select the currency type for the cost value the passenger is claiming by pointing to the currency code in the drop-down list and clicking on the selected currency.

Photos

 

You have the option of downloading digital photos of the damage bag to your computer and then uploading these photos to the Damage Baggage Report.
